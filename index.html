Home Runs per Handedness
Player's Average Batting Performance

Left handed players perform more home runs

According to the data, the left handed players have an average of 56 homeruns, compared to 43 and 32 of right handed and both handed players, respectively.

Both handed players have a better batting performance

Both handed players presented an average batting performance of 0.21, they are closely followed by left handed players, with an average performance of 0.20, finally, right handed players had a 0.18 average performance.

Player Efficiency per Height

Shorter players are the best !

According to the data, the players 57'' tall had the best values for home runs and battling performance (with 100 and 0.26 values for the average number of home runs and the average battling performance, respectively).

Player Efficiency per Weight

Heavier players are better players

The chart shows that the better values for the average number of home runs are concentrated near the players wich weights around 209 lbs. This may be a consequence of player's muscular mass. In the other hand, we could not see any relation with weight and battling performance. The scatter plot is very diffuse, not suggesting any distribution.

Conclusions

Data Analysis Conclusions

As we could see analysing the charts, player's handedness, height and weight really have an influence on its average performance. The most affected variable, according to players attributes, was the average number of home runs. Left handed players, shorter and heavier, according to the data, presented an expressive higher number of home runs, compared with other players. In contrast, both handed players, taller and lighter players, performed worse (again, according to the analysed data).
